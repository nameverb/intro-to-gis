Therapist Deserts

Exploring locations of therapists in the contiguous United States relative to estimated population.

By Drew Stanley



Introduction

While much of our national conversation focuses on physical health, we often neglect to discuss the importance of access to mental health in the United States. According to the National Alliance on Mental Illness, millions of adults in the US face some form of mental illness, and yet 60% of them “didn’t receive mental health services in the previous year.”1
Social stigma, inadequate (or nonexistent) health insurance, or unaffordable copays are likely contributors to people not seeking help, but what about those people who live in areas where there are no mental health professionals to go to?
This project focuses on “therapist deserts,” or areas in the contiguous United States that don’t have a therapist or other mental health professional within 25 miles of where people live.
